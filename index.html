About Me

I am Tianhan Ling, a graduate student in Business Analytics at the University of Southern California. I completed my undergraduate studies at the University of California, Irvine, majoring in Mathematics and minoring in Statistics. This background equipped me with a solid foundation in linear algebra and probability, preparing me for advanced data science studies. While at CMU, I delved into exploratory data analysis, familiarized myself with various machine learning models, and learned techniques for visualizing data and handling large datasets. Currently, at the Marshall School of Business, I collaborate within a diverse, cross-functional team, further enhancing my interpersonal and communication skills.

With a background in biological imaging, I worked as a data analyst in a cross-functional team. My colleague specialized in data mining, while another was a proficient full-stack engineer. During my tenure, I honed my skills in Tableau, presenting findings biweekly, which significantly improved my communication and presentation abilities. Beyond this, my professional journey has spanned the insurance and advertising sectors. These diverse experiences have equipped me with the agility to seamlessly integrate into new teams and fostered my collaborative spirit. A notable accomplishment was my involvement in setting up Publicis Groupe's advertising price database. Collaborating with various advertising teams provided insights into diverse data formats and crucial aspects of the advertising world. This comprehensive understanding enabled me to efficiently organize and sort through over 10,000 data points in a mere three days.

During my time at university, I primarily used Python and R for programming. In both of my internships, Excel was an essential tool. Furthermore, I took the initiative to learn SQL and Tableau on my own, culminating in a visualization project that delved into the correlation between COVID-19 fatalities and vaccination rates. I believe these experiences and skills have equipped me well for future career endeavors.
